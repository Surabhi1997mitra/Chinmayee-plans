What changes starting now
1. Post-meal walks (10–15 min)
After lunch and dinner, walk slowly around your home or outside. This single habit prevents blood sugar spikes and addresses your low HDL. Non-negotiable.
2. Oil brush + spray bottle
Cooking at home is great — but invisible oil adds hundreds of calories. Brush oil thin on the pan instead of pouring. Use a spray bottle for rotis. Cheapest, highest-impact swap you can make.
3. Protein at every meal
Breakfast: eggs + roti. Lunch: chicken/fish + dal + rice. Dinner: chicken/paneer + sabzi. No meal without protein.
4. Low-GI carbs only
Brown rice 2–3×/week. All other days: rotis (wheat, bajra, jowar, ragi). No white rice, no refined flour, no bread. Your blood sugar will thank you.

All workouts follow this format
Warm-up · 3–5 min
Arm circles, leg swings, slow walking or light jogging in place. Just to get blood flowing.
Main workout · 5 exercises · 20 min
Do each exercise back-to-back with minimal rest (30–45 seconds between). No equipment needed — just your body.
Cool-down & stretch · 5 min
Gentle stretches for muscles you just worked. Hold each 20–30 seconds.
Monday · Lower Body
Glutes, legs, lower back strength.
Warm-up
Arm circles 20 sec · Leg swings 20 sec · Walk in place 1 min
Main Workout · Do all 5 exercises back-to-back, 2–3 rounds
1
Glute Bridges
12 reps × 2–3 rounds · Lie on back, knees bent, push hips up. Squeeze glutes at top. Lower slowly.
2
Bodyweight Squats
12 reps × 2–3 rounds · Feet shoulder-width apart. Lower down like sitting in a chair. Keep chest up.
3
Standing Reverse Lunges
10 reps each leg × 2–3 rounds · Step back with one leg, lower body, push back to standing. Repeat on other side. Good for back safety.
4
Calf Raises
15 reps × 2–3 rounds · Stand, raise up on tiptoes. Lower. Repeat. Can hold onto a wall for balance.
5
Leg Raises (lying)
10 reps × 2–3 rounds · Lie on back, legs straight. Raise legs to 45°, lower without touching ground. Gentle on lower back.
Cool-down · 5 min
Quad stretch (30 sec each leg) · Hamstring stretch (30 sec each) · Glute stretch (30 sec each) · Child's pose (45 sec)
Tuesday · Upper Body
Arms, chest, shoulders, back.
Warm-up
Arm circles 30 sec each direction · Shoulder rolls · Wall slides 1 min
Main Workout · Do all 5 exercises back-to-back, 2–3 rounds
1
Wall Push-ups or Incline Push-ups
10 reps × 2–3 rounds · Hands on wall (easier) or on a table/couch (harder). Bend elbows, push back. Start easy, progress later.
2
Tricep Dips
10 reps × 2–3 rounds · Use a chair or couch. Hands behind, lower body down, push back up.
3
Plank Hold
10–60 sec holds × 2–3 rounds · Full plank position. Start with 10 sec if needed, work toward 30 sec, then 1 min. Keep back straight, no sagging hips.
4
Lateral Raise with 2 bottles
12 reps × 2–3 rounds · Hold 2 identical bottles (water bottles filled, or cans). Raise arms to shoulder height, lower. Builds shoulder strength.
5
Dead Bug (alternating)
10 reps each side × 2–3 rounds · Lie on back, arms up, knees bent at 90°. Lower opposite arm and leg slowly, bring back up. Slow and controlled. Great for core and back.
Cool-down · 5 min
Chest stretch (30 sec) · Shoulder stretch (30 sec each) · Tricep stretch (30 sec each) · Child's pose (45 sec)
Thursday · Full Body
Simple circuit — everything in one session.
Warm-up
Jumping jacks 20 sec · High knees 20 sec · Arm circles 20 sec
Main Workout · Do all 5 exercises back-to-back, 2 rounds
1
Burpees (simplified)
8 reps × 2 rounds · Step back into plank (don't jump), step feet back in, stand up. No jump if it's hard — step instead.
2
Mountain Climbers
20 reps (each leg) × 2 rounds · Plank position, bring knee to chest alternately. Steady pace.
3
Jump Squats or Regular Squats
12 reps × 2 rounds · Jump if you can (builds cardio + power). Or regular squats if knees feel sensitive.
4
Push-ups (wall or incline)
8–10 reps × 2 rounds · Same as Tuesday. Do your best version.
5
Glute Bridges
15 reps × 2 rounds · Finish strong. Activate glutes.
Cool-down · 5 min
Walk slowly 1 min · Full body stretch (legs, arms, chest, back) — 4 min
Friday · Cardio
Running or outdoor walking — your choice.
Pick one
Option A — Running/Jogging: 25–30 min at a pace where you can talk but not sing. Start slow, build week on week.

Option B — Brisk Walking: 30–40 min at a steady pace. Same cardio benefit, easier on joints.

Option C — Mixed: 5 min walk warm-up → 15 min jog/run → 5 min walk cool-down

🚶 This can be outdoors or on a treadmill. Trekking readiness comes from consistent cardio. This day is critical.
Rest days & active recovery
Wednesday · Rest Day
Full rest or very light — gentle yoga, stretching, walking 30 min. Your body recovers here. Don't skip recovery thinking "more is better."
Saturday · Active Rest
Light walk, yoga, or mobility work. Nothing intense. This is recovery day, not training day.
Sunday · Complete Rest
Off. No workouts. Focus on sleep, meals, hydration. Mental break matters.
Progression — how to get harder
Week 1–2: Learn the movements
Focus on form, not speed. Do 2 rounds. Feel the muscles. No rush.
Week 3–4: Increase reps
Same exercises, more reps. 12 → 14 → 15. Or add a 3rd round.
Week 5+: Harder versions
Wall push-ups → Incline push-ups → Floor push-ups (if ready). Regular squats → Jump squats. Plank on knees → Full plank. Progress at your pace, no rush.
Back pain note: These exercises are designed to strengthen glutes and improve posture — which actually fixes lower back pain. If any exercise causes sharp pain (not just muscle fatigue), skip it and tell me in the check-in.
